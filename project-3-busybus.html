BusyBus

BusyBus is a mobile app designed to make riding the bus easier.

View Prototype
SUMMARY

People commonly have frustrations and confusion with public transit. BusyBus is an app designed to simplify bus transit and provide real-time updates to riders.

ROLES

User Research
UX Design
HTML/CSS

TOOLS

Figma
Marvel App

The Problem

Bus riders want to know which bus will be arriving at their stop next and when it will be arriving. They are most commonly complaining about the bus stop at Washington and State, which has seven bus lines serving the stop.

The Solution

The BusyBus app simplifies bus transit by showing which bus is coming to the user's stop next along with providing real-time updates on service delays.

User Research

The project began with researching how people are currently using transit apps including what they like and dislike about the apps they use.

Most common frustration

Buses are unreliable or not on time and they take too long.

Most desired feature

Bus times/schedules and bus stop locations.

66.7%

of users who take the bus said that they would take the bus more often if they had a more reliable app.

View User Survey

Competitive Analysis

I completed a competitive analysis using two of the most popular transit apps, Google Maps and CityMapper. For BusyBus to enter this market, they would need to take the best features from both of these apps, that apply to bus transit.

Most importantly, the app needs to provide the most accurate real-time updates on bus times and delays.

Google Maps has very strong brand awareness along with including over 220 countries and territories mapped. They provide turn-by-turn navigation that gives accurate route options and guidance for travel by car, public transit, walking, biking, and ridesharing. It also includes business information such as contact information, hours, and reviews.

CityMapper is a transit app geared towards savvy commuters who use public transportation daily. The app has a very user-friendly design and shows you very specific details about your commute while also integrating with more travel options such as Citibike.

Paper prototypes

I sketched paper prototypes to show the following app features:

  • Searching for a destination
  • Viewing their destination bus stop, Washington & State
  • Scrolling through bus options that will take them to their destination, showing them the bus details:
    • Bus line
    • Arrival time
    • Bus stop location & distance

Usability Testing

I asked users to complete the task of looking up which bus will take them to Washington & State Streets and what time it will be arriving at the bus stop.

One issue that I observed while testing was that some of the information under the bus details was not as clearly understood by users as it should have been, such as the location and distance for the bus stop.

Information Architecture

During the design process, one of the most important aspects was how the information on the app is organized so it can easily be understood for the user.

These photos show how the design of the bus details evolved over the course of the project.

Design Process

The design for the BusyBus app changed a lot over the course of this project. I started with a basic sketch, then created a hifi mockup in Figma, followed by an HTML/CSS version where multiple changes to the design and information architecture occurred.

An unexpected design change came up when the client provided data that included buses organized by directions, so this section was added in to the bus list as well, showing categories such as “Eastbound” and “Southbound”.

Final Prototype

Following the Figma prototype, I created a working version of this design using HTML and CSS. The majority of the design changes occurred within the bus list, which shows how important the information architecture is for this app.
